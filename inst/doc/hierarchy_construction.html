Prepare the data

Meng Wang

2019-01-03

The purpose of this vignette demonstrates how to prepare data for ReSET. First, we read in and preprocess two 10X scRNAseq datasets containing around 3000 and 4000 Pan T cells from a single normal donor. The two datasets are available here and here. We used preprocess_data function to filter and normalize the data. PCA were performed as well. The output is a Seurat object.

We performed graph-based clustering using Seurat and construct a tree of the clusters using hierarchical clustering on the mean of gene expression. Here is the dendrogram for T3K dataset.

T3K_data <- Seurat::FindClusters(T3K_data, reduction.type = "pca", dims.use = 1:12, resolution = 2, print.output = 0) # 12 clusters
T3K_hierarchy <- ReSET::construct_hierarchy(T3K_data@scale.data, membership = T3K_data@ident, mean)
stats:::plot.dendrogram(T3K_hierarchy$dend)

And here is the dendrogram for T4K dataset.

T4K_data <- Seurat::FindClusters(T4K_data, reduction.type = "pca", dims.use = 1:12, resolution = 2, print.output = 0) # 13 clusters
T4K_hierarchy <- ReSET::construct_hierarchy(T4K_data@scale.data, membership = T4K_data@ident, mean)
stats:::plot.dendrogram(T4K_hierarchy$dend)
